Day 1: Sign my contract
=================

__23 Feb 2019, Singapore__. I visted a tuition center in Singapore today. I applied for a teaching position. To my surprise, it was 'too easy'. The HR manager only asked for what subjects I studied, without asking for my marks. The 'big boss' there seemed very happy with me there, continuously laughed and asked if I could invite my friends to join them. It was exactly what I would imagine for people selling 'snake oil'.

Well, in fact, I do feel 'scammed'. My hourly pay is not very good, but well. Due to some administrative matters, I don't have many choices.

So i signed the contract. My first job did not feel like a major feat of success, but rather feeling like felling into a trick scam.

Hope my negativity with the job will soon be replaced with the positive ones. Will contact my Physics teacher asking whether I can be useful to him on Fridays.

Day 2: Got my place to stay
===================

__24 Feb 2019, Singapore__. After 2 tiring days looking for rooms to rent, I finally chose a place to stay at. The rent is okay compared to market price, I think. There is a direct bus from there to where I work so it's quite convenient.

My first exxperience finding a place to stay was quite fun. Met different kinds of people, with their own unique backgrounds. Some were young couples with small children, some were middlemen, others were singles. Some cases were hard to talk with because there were clearly in sensitive circumstances. But most of them were friendly and welcoming. Well, everyone can be 'theatrical' a bit so hard to tell.

Hope things go well with my stay here.

Had a short chat with my local friend's mother. She was very nice and welcoming me to stay, but of course I don't want to abuse her generosity. I went out and bought Vietnamese stuff at local supermarket to gift my friend's family. Haha.

Day 3: First day of work
=========================

__25 Feb 2019, Singapore__. My first day of work at a tuition center. The first few hours were boring and tiring when I had to read and learn American math. However, as tiime went by, I met some interesting full-time tutors there, explaining to me how to teach a specific topic to a student. It was quite insightful. I could never imagine a very systematic and easy-to-follow way to explain basic topics. I learned something about teaching.

Today ended late. It was tiring. I hope I will get used to it soon. Below is the view outside my workplace.

Day 4: Second day of work
========================

__26 Feb 2019, Singapore__. I think I kind of get used to the flow of work now. One of the tutors here taught me another topic today, but I barely listened. At least I will have to do work before the students arrive. After that, most tutors are busy, so I'm left to do my own work.

I'm the last one still under training. The rest have finished their 32 hours, so I will be alone from 1130 to 330. Guess it's fine liddat.

Day 5: Packed lunch
=====================

Today was tiring. Hope tomorrow will be better.

Day 8: A Vietnamese in Singapore
====================

__02 Mar 2019, Singapore__. I met two of my friends today. It was a strange feeling. Be there but not be there. It seemed that we were operating at different frequencies: I couldn't understand their worries in life, and they could not realize my concerns I faced. We were close classmates, but nothing holds us together anymore. 

On my bus trip back, I cried. I cried because I felt so lonely and unfair. My life is shattered into pieces right now. I don't know what the future holds. I don't know what's the point of the past. It becomes harder to find someone I can talk to. I missed my mom. I wanted to talk to her so bad. To hear her voice and her wisdom.

Today at work was boring. I didn't do anything, just sitting there for 9 hours.

Day 9: My first student
===================

__03 Mar 2019, Singapore__. I had my first student today. He seemed okay with basic math operations, if not adept. But he didn't have much confidence in his answer, keep saying "This is probably wrong" or something along the line. He knew his stuff well, so I only needed to give him the quizzes and explain to him again parts that he was weaker at. Overall I would say my first experience teaching here was not bad at all.

The cat greeted me today. He asked for food, but I couldn't give any to him. Sad boy. He has been pretty well-behaved so far.

I received some updates about mom today. I do miss her. I told myself on my way back from work to treat her a good meal with my first salary.
